The NEWBIE Guide

Tipps and Tricks for our Newbies

For the very new Ones

Welcome to The ETH Guide! Are you new to ETH? Then you are at the right place! Not only made for newbies, this website should be a helping hand from students to students, because let's be honest, every one of us gets confused once in a while at ETH, am I right? First, you might want to find out to what Study Association you belong to. For this, search for your course here. To find the website of your student association, click here.

How to start

The first thing you might want to do is to login to MyStudies and get your timetable. You should by now have set a new password or at least know the one given to you by ETH. Once you're logged in, you will have to press the button saying "Functions".

Next, you will be clicking on "Course registration".
.
The next step will be individual for each one of you. You'll have to choose your programme. As an example I've chosen to show "Computer Science Bachelor's Degree Programme".

There's no need to fill out more then this, just hit the "Search" button and you'll get a list of the courses you will be taking. Save your progress and you're ready to see your "Course Schedule". You should have the option to click on it at the top of the page.

At first, it will all seem quite chaotic. Don't worry. At the top of the table click on "Timetable". This should look more familiar ;).



In the list, you'll only have to tick some of the courses, but this is not quite important yet... On the timetable, there's a number on top of every course followed by one letter: "V" which comes from "Vorlesung", german for Lecture. Those normally take place in relatively large lecture rooms.
